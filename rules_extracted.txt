UNIFIED RULE ENGINE
for Insurance RAG Chatbot
Purpose
This document defines the complete, unified decision logic for an AI-powered insurance advisory chatbot. It merges the Profile & Needs Assessment Rule Engine with the Product Matching Engine into a single authoritative reference. The engine operates across five sequential phases, each with clearly defined inputs, conditions, and outputs that can be directly encoded into a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system.

Phase 2 — Regulatory Filters & Compliance Logic
Before any product is surfaced, the engine applies mandatory IRDAI and statutory filters. These rules are non-negotiable and override all preference-based logic.
2.1  Pre-Existing Disease (PED) Rules
IRDAI 2024 Health Insurance Regulations — Mandatory Enforcement
• Insurers CANNOT refuse health insurance coverage on grounds of severe illness (Cancer, Heart Disease, AIDS, Renal Failure, etc.)
• 
Maximum waiting period for PEDs:
 3 years (enforced at policy inception)
• 
5-Year Moratorium Rule:
 After 5 continuous years of coverage, no claim can be rejected on grounds of PED non-disclosure unless actual fraud is proven
• Chatbot must proactively inform users of these rights before presenting any product
2.2  Life & Term Insurance Compliance
Suicide Exclusion:
 All life/term plans exclude suicide within the first 12 months — flag at point of recommendation
Hazardous Occupation / Sports:
 Flag exclusions for aviation, hazardous sports (specifically Max Life Smart Secure Plus)
DUI / No License:
 Flag motor policy exclusions for driving under the influence or without a valid licence
2.3  Motor Insurance Mandates
Newly Purchased Vehicle:
 Engine enforces IRDAI mandate — 3-year Third-Party (TP) + 1-year Own Damage (OD) comprehensive policy is legally required
Existing Vehicle with long-term TP policy:
 Route exclusively to Standalone OD policies (e.g., Bajaj Allianz Motor OD)

Phase 5 — Chatbot Compliance & AI Transparency
Throughout every phase, the chatbot must operate within IRDAI’s AI and mis-selling guidelines.
AI Transparency Labels:
 Every chatbot response must be labelled as AI-generated advice, not a certified financial recommendation
Suitability Assessment:
 A formal suitability check must be completed before finalising any product recommendation, in accordance with IRDAI Point of Sale regulations
Mis-selling Prevention:
 The engine must never suppress negative product attributes (waiting periods, exclusions, lock-ins) to improve conversion metrics
Data Privacy:
 User health and financial data collected during profile ingestion must be handled per DPDP Act 2023 requirements
Human Handoff Trigger:
 If the user expresses dissatisfaction, legal distress, or asks for a certified advisor, the engine must immediately surface a human agent escalation path
